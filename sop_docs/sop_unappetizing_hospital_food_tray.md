### SOP Title
Unappetizing Hospital Food Tray Standard Operating Procedure
### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, particularly those in the Dietary department, Ward Nurses, and other relevant personnel who handle patient food trays. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure that patients receive appetizing and nutritious food, addressing concerns related to the presentation, quality, and timeliness of hospital food.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators may trigger this SOP:
- Cold food in tray
- Half-empty or incomplete tray
- Dirty utensils
- Wrong food type for patient condition
Additionally, patient complaints or feedback regarding unappetizing food should also initiate this procedure.

### Severity Policy
All incidents related to unappetizing hospital food trays are classified as low severity. However, if a patient's condition is affected or if there is a risk of physical harm due to inadequate nutrition or food safety, the severity should be reassessed and escalated accordingly.

### Department Routing
Initial reports of unappetizing hospital food trays should be directed to the Ward Nurse on duty. The Ward Nurse will then notify the Dietary department and ensure that the issue is addressed promptly.

### First Response Actions
Upon receiving a report of an unappetizing hospital food tray:
1. **Replace meal if possible**: Offer the patient a replacement meal that meets their dietary needs and preferences.
2. **Note in patient record**: Document the incident in the patient's medical record, including the nature of the complaint and the actions taken to resolve it.
3. **Report to kitchen supervisor**: Inform the kitchen supervisor about the issue to prevent future occurrences.
4. **Review diet chart accuracy**: Verify that the patient's diet chart is accurate and up-to-date to ensure that meals are prepared according to their specific needs.

### Escalation Chain
If the issue cannot be resolved by the Ward Nurse and the Dietary department, it should be escalated to:
1. **Dietitian**: For advice on dietary adjustments or special requirements.
2. **Kitchen Supervisor**: To address kitchen-related issues such as food preparation, presentation, and timing.
3. **Patient Services Manager**: For complaints that require a higher level of intervention or for patterns of recurring issues.

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for addressing unappetizing hospital food tray issues is **3 hours** from the time the complaint is received. This ensures that patients receive timely attention to their concerns and that issues are resolved before they significantly impact patient satisfaction or care.

### Human Review Triggers
The following situations require human review:
- Recurring complaints from the same patient or ward.
- Patterns of similar complaints across different wards or patient groups.
- Instances where the patient's condition may be compromised due to inadequate nutrition or food safety concerns.

### Audit Fields
For auditing and quality improvement purposes, the following fields should be monitored and recorded:
- Date and time of complaint
- Nature of complaint (e.g., cold food, wrong diet type)
- Actions taken to resolve the issue
- Outcome (e.g., meal replaced, patient satisfied)
- Escalation details (if applicable)
Regular review of these audit fields will help in identifying trends, improving the hospital's food service quality, and enhancing patient satisfaction.
