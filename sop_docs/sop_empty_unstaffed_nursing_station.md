### SOP Title
Empty or Unstaffed Nursing Station Procedure
==============================================

### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all nursing stations within the hospital. It outlines the steps to be taken when a nursing station is found to be empty or unstaffed, ensuring patient safety and prompt response to emergencies.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators trigger this SOP:
- Empty nursing counter
- Unanswered call bells
- No staff visible in the ward
- Patients unattended in visible distress

### Severity Policy
All instances of an empty or unstaffed nursing station are considered critical, as they pose a significant risk to patient safety and well-being.

### Department Routing
Upon identification of an empty or unstaffed nursing station, the incident is immediately routed to the Nursing Department for prompt action.

### First Response Actions
1. **Deploy Standby Nurse Immediately**: A standby nurse is deployed to the empty nursing station to ensure immediate patient care and supervision.
2. **Review Shift Allocation**: The Nursing Supervisor reviews the shift allocation to identify any gaps or oversights that led to the nursing station being unstaffed.
3. **Conduct Inquiry**: If a life-threatening gap occurred, an inquiry is conducted to determine the cause and implement measures to prevent future occurrences.

### Escalation Chain
In case of severe consequences or if the situation is not resolved promptly, the escalation chain is as follows:
1. Nursing Supervisor
2. Duty Medical Officer
3. Medical Superintendent
4. Hospital Administrator

### SLA Targets
The target response time for deploying a standby nurse to an empty or unstaffed nursing station is **1 hour**.

### Human Review Triggers
The following scenarios require a human review:
- Any instance of physical harm to a patient due to an empty or unstaffed nursing station.
- Repeated occurrences of empty or unstaffed nursing stations within a short timeframe.
- Inquiries or complaints from patients or their families regarding the absence of nursing staff.

### Audit Fields
The following fields are audited for compliance and improvement:
- Time of incident report
- Time of standby nurse deployment
- Cause of the empty or unstaffed nursing station
- Actions taken to prevent future occurrences
- Outcome of the incident, including any harm to patients or staff responses.
