### SOP Title
Overflowing Hospital Trash (Outside) Management Procedure
==============================================

### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all instances of overflowing hospital trash outside the hospital premises. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure prompt and effective management of overflowing trash to maintain a clean, safe, and healthy environment for patients, visitors, and staff.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators will trigger the implementation of this SOP:
- Waste spilling out of bins
- Garbage on floor or surroundings
- Mixed biomedical and general waste
- Visible insects or pests near waste
- Uncovered waste containers

### Severity Policy
All instances of overflowing hospital trash outside will be classified as medium severity, requiring immediate attention to prevent potential health hazards and environmental pollution.

### Department Routing
All reports of overflowing hospital trash outside will be routed to the Waste Management department for immediate action.

### First Response Actions
Upon receiving a report, the Housekeeping Staff will take the following first response actions:
1. Clear the overflow and arrange for additional bins if necessary.
2. Segregate biomedical waste properly to prevent mixing with general waste.
3. Schedule an urgent waste pickup to prevent further accumulation.

### Escalation Chain
If the issue is not resolved within the specified SLA or if it requires additional support, the escalation chain will be as follows:
1. Housekeeping Supervisor
2. Biomedical Waste Officer
3. Facilities Manager
4. Hospital Administrator

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for overflowing hospital trash outside is 2 hours from the time of reporting.

### Human Review Triggers
The following situations will trigger a human review:
- Reports of physical harm or potential health risks associated with the overflowing trash.
- Instances where the evidence provided is unclear or partial, requiring further investigation.
- Situations where the consistency score is below the average, indicating potential discrepancies in the report.

### Audit Fields
The following fields will be audited to ensure compliance with this SOP:
- Time of report receipt
- Time of first response action
- Time of resolution
- Escalation chain activation (if applicable)
- Human review trigger (if applicable)
- Consistency score
- Evidence clarity
- Physical harm or potential health risks (if applicable)
