### SOP Title
Dirty Hospital Bathroom Triage and Resolution Procedure
### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, particularly Housekeeping and Facilities Management teams, responsible for maintaining the cleanliness and hygiene of hospital bathrooms. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure prompt identification, reporting, and resolution of dirty hospital bathroom issues to prevent the spread of infections and maintain a safe environment for patients, visitors, and staff.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators trigger the implementation of this SOP:
- Visible stains or waste on surfaces
- Standing water on floor
- Broken fixtures
- Strong unpleasant smell
- Empty soap dispensers
Additionally, reports from patients, visitors, or staff regarding unclean conditions, foul odors, or broken fittings in hospital bathrooms will also trigger this SOP.

### Severity Policy
All reports of dirty hospital bathrooms are considered high severity due to the potential risk of infection and the importance of maintaining a clean environment for patient care and recovery.

### Department Routing
All initial reports will be routed to the Housekeeping department for immediate action. Depending on the nature of the issue (e.g., broken fixtures, overflowing drains), the Facilities Management department may also be involved.

### First Response Actions
Upon receipt of a report, the following first response actions will be taken:
1. **Immediate Assessment**: A Housekeeping staff member will immediately assess the bathroom condition to confirm the report.
2. **Cleaning and Sanitisation**: The bathroom will be thoroughly cleaned and sanitised, including all fixtures and surfaces.
3. **Repair of Broken Fittings**: Any broken fixtures (e.g., taps, toilets) will be repaired or replaced as necessary.
4. **Restocking of Toiletries**: Soap dispensers, toilet paper, and other essential toiletries will be restocked.
5. **Inspection of Other Bathrooms**: The Housekeeping staff will inspect other nearby bathrooms to ensure they are clean and well-maintained.

### Escalation Chain
If the issue cannot be resolved by the Housekeeping staff or if it involves more complex repairs, the following escalation chain will be followed:
1. **Housekeeping Supervisor**: For issues requiring additional resources or more complex cleaning procedures.
2. **Facilities Manager**: For repairs of broken fixtures or maintenance issues.
3. **Infection Control Officer**: If there's a risk of infection spread or if the bathroom's condition poses a significant health risk.
4. **Hospital Administrator**: For severe cases or if the issue reflects a systemic problem with hospital maintenance or cleanliness.

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for dirty hospital bathroom reports is 1 hour. This means that from the time a report is received, the bathroom should be cleaned, sanitised, and any necessary repairs or restocking should be completed within 60 minutes.

### Human Review Triggers
The following situations will trigger a human review:
- Any report of a dirty hospital bathroom that is not resolved within the 1-hour SLA target.
- Reports indicating potential health risks or infections related to bathroom cleanliness.
- Recurring issues in the same bathroom, suggesting a systemic maintenance or cleaning problem.

### Audit Fields
The following fields will be audited to ensure compliance with this SOP:
- Time of report receipt
- Time of initial assessment
- Time of completion of cleaning and sanitisation
- Time of completion of any repairs or restocking
- Confirmation of bathroom inspection and clearance by Housekeeping staff
- Escalation actions taken (if any)
- Final resolution time and confirmation of issue closure
