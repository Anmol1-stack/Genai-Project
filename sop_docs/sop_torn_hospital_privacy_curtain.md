### SOP Title
Torn Hospital Privacy Curtain Standard Operating Procedure

### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, particularly Housekeeping and Ward Nurses, responsible for maintaining patient privacy and addressing maintenance issues within the hospital premises.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators will trigger the implementation of this SOP:
- Torn fabric with visible gaps
- Missing curtain panel
- Dangling or fallen curtain rod
- Stained or discoloured fabric

### Severity Policy
All incidents of torn hospital privacy curtains will be classified as low severity. However, if the issue compromises patient privacy or dignity, it may be reassessed based on the specific circumstances.

### Department Routing
All reports of torn hospital privacy curtains will be routed to the Housekeeping department for immediate attention and resolution.

### First Response Actions
Upon receiving a report, the Ward Nurse or the first responder will:
1. Assess the situation to ensure patient privacy and dignity are maintained.
2. Provide a temporary solution if possible (e.g., arranging a temporary screen).
3. Log the issue in the maintenance register.
4. Check adjacent bays for any similar issues.

### Escalation Chain
If the issue cannot be resolved promptly, it will be escalated to:
1. Ward In-Charge
2. Housekeeping Supervisor
3. Facilities Manager

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for all torn hospital privacy curtain incidents is 8 hours.

### Human Review Triggers
The following will trigger a human review:
- Any report of a torn curtain that compromises patient privacy or dignity.
- Failure to resolve the issue within the SLA target time.
- Recurring issues in the same area.

### Audit Fields
The following fields will be audited for compliance and effectiveness of this SOP:
- Time of report
- Time of resolution
- Department responsible for resolution
- Actions taken
- Outcome (resolved, escalated, pending)
