### SOP Title
Crowded Hospital Waiting Room Management

### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, particularly Reception / OPD Staff, Administration, and other departments involved in managing the hospital waiting room. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure a safe, comfortable, and efficient waiting experience for patients and visitors.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators trigger the implementation of this SOP:
- People standing due to no seats
- Patients sitting on floor
- No visible queue management
- Blocked wheelchair access
- Visible distress on patient faces

### Severity Policy
All incidents related to a crowded hospital waiting room are classified as medium severity. This classification is based on the potential for patient discomfort, anxiety, and risk of infection transmission in crowded areas.

### Department Routing
Incidents reported under this category are primarily routed to the Administration department for resolution. The Reception / OPD Staff are the first responders and are responsible for initial assessment and action.

### First Response Actions
Upon identification of a crowded waiting room, the first response actions include:
- Deploying crowd management strategies
- Opening additional counters to reduce wait times
- Arranging temporary seating to accommodate more patients
- Assessing queue flow to optimize patient movement

### Escalation Chain
If the situation is not resolved within the initial response, the escalation chain is as follows:
1. OPD In-Charge
2. Administrative Officer
3. Medical Superintendent
4. Hospital Director

Each level of escalation is responsible for reviewing the situation, providing additional resources if necessary, and ensuring that the waiting room conditions are improved to meet patient needs and safety standards.

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for addressing a crowded hospital waiting room is 2 hours. This timeframe is crucial to minimize patient discomfort and prevent potential health risks associated with overcrowding.

### Human Review Triggers
The following situations require human review:
- Reports of physical harm to patients or staff due to overcrowding
- Evidence of significant distress or discomfort among patients
- Failure to resolve the issue within the specified SLA target
- Reports of unhygienic conditions or risk of infection transmission

### Audit Fields
The following fields are subject to audit to ensure compliance with this SOP:
- Time of incident report
- Initial response time
- Actions taken to resolve the issue
- Escalation history (if applicable)
- Resolution time
- Patient feedback (if available)

Regular audits will help identify areas for improvement in the management of crowded hospital waiting rooms, ensuring that patients receive a safe and comfortable experience.
