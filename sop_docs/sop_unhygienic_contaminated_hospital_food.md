### SOP Title
Hospital Food Contamination Triage and Response Procedure
### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all instances of reported unhygienic or contaminated hospital food. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure prompt identification, reporting, and resolution of food contamination incidents to prevent patient harm and maintain high standards of food safety and hygiene within the hospital.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators trigger this SOP:
- Visible foreign objects in food
- Mould or discolouration
- Raw or bloody meat
- Off smell from food
- Insect in food tray
Any hospital staff, patient, or visitor who observes these indicators should immediately report the incident.

### Severity Policy
All reports of contaminated hospital food are considered high severity due to the potential risk of patient harm and infection. Immediate action is required to address the situation and prevent further contamination.

### Department Routing
All reports of contaminated hospital food are routed to the Dietary department for immediate attention and action. Infection Control is also alerted to assess the situation and provide guidance on preventing the spread of potential infections.

### First Response Actions
Upon receiving a report of contaminated hospital food, the following first response actions are taken:
1. **Immediately remove contaminated food**: Remove the contaminated food from the patient's tray or the serving area to prevent consumption.
2. **Preserve sample for testing**: Preserve a sample of the contaminated food for laboratory testing to identify the cause of contamination.
3. **Alert Infection Control**: Notify the Infection Control team to assess the situation and provide guidance on infection prevention and control measures.
4. **Inspect kitchen batch**: Inspect the kitchen batch from which the contaminated food originated to identify any other potentially contaminated food.
5. **Monitor affected patients**: Monitor patients who may have consumed contaminated food for signs of infection or illness.

### Escalation Chain
The escalation chain for reports of contaminated hospital food is as follows:
1. Ward Nurse
2. Infection Control Officer
3. Kitchen Supervisor
4. Medical Superintendent
5. Hospital Administrator

### SLA Targets
The target response time for reports of contaminated hospital food is 2 hours. This ensures that the situation is addressed promptly to prevent further contamination and potential patient harm.

### Human Review Triggers
The following triggers require human review:
- All reports of contaminated hospital food
- Any instance where a patient shows signs of infection or illness after consuming hospital food
- When laboratory test results indicate the presence of harmful pathogens in the food sample

### Audit Fields
The following fields are audited for all reports of contaminated hospital food:
- Date and time of report
- Description of contamination
- Actions taken in response to the report
- Laboratory test results (if applicable)
- Outcome for patients who may have consumed contaminated food
- Corrective actions taken to prevent future incidents of contamination
