### SOP Title
Hospital Bed Maintenance and Repair Standard Operating Procedure

### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, particularly the Maintenance Team and Ward Nurses, responsible for ensuring the safety and functionality of hospital beds. The purpose of this SOP is to outline the steps to be taken in the event of a broken hospital bed, ensuring prompt repair or replacement to maintain patient safety and comfort.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators will trigger the implementation of this SOP:
- Bent or cracked frame
- Exposed metal edges
- Missing or torn mattress
- Sagging or tilted bed surface
- Loose or detached railings

### Severity Policy
All broken hospital beds are considered high-severity incidents due to the potential risk to patient safety and comfort. Immediate action is required to isolate the bed and arrange for repair or replacement.

### Department Routing
- Initial reports will be routed to the Maintenance Team for immediate assessment and action.
- Ward Nurses will be responsible for the initial response, including isolating the bed and notifying the Maintenance Team.

### First Response Actions
Upon notification of a broken hospital bed:
1. Isolate the bed immediately to prevent further use.
2. Arrange for a replacement bed if available.
3. Log the incident in the maintenance register, including details of the issue and the actions taken.

### Escalation Chain
In the event of delays in repair or replacement, or if the issue is not resolved satisfactorily, the following escalation chain will be followed:
1. Ward In-Charge
2. Maintenance Supervisor
3. Hospital Engineering Head
4. Hospital Administrator

### SLA Targets
The target resolution time for broken hospital bed incidents is 4 hours. This includes isolation of the bed, arrangement for a replacement if possible, and commencement of repair or replacement procedures.

### Human Review Triggers
The following will trigger a human review of the incident:
- Any incident where physical harm has occurred or could have occurred due to the broken bed.
- Incidents where the evidence clarity is unclear or partial, requiring further investigation.
- Repeated incidents in the same area or with the same type of bed, indicating a potential systemic issue.

### Audit Fields
The following fields will be audited for compliance with this SOP:
- Time from incident report to bed isolation
- Time from incident report to commencement of repair or replacement
- Completion of maintenance register entry for each incident
- Escalation chain adherence for unresolved issues
- Review of human review triggers to ensure appropriateness and timeliness of response.
