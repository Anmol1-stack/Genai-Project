### SOP Title
Rodent Infestation Triage and Response Procedure
### Scope
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) applies to all hospital staff, patients, and visitors who encounter or report rodent infestations within the hospital premises. The purpose of this SOP is to ensure prompt and effective response to rodent infestations, minimizing the risk of disease transmission and maintaining a clean and safe environment.

### Trigger Indicators
The following visual indicators will trigger the rodent infestation response procedure:
* Live rodent sighted
* Droppings on floor or shelves
* Gnaw marks on materials
* Nest materials found
* Disturbed food packaging

### Severity Policy
All reported rodent infestations will be considered critical and will be addressed immediately. The severity of the infestation will be assessed by the Housekeeping Supervisor and Pest Control team, who will determine the necessary response and treatment.

### Department Routing
All rodent infestation reports will be routed to the Pest Control department, with immediate notification to the Housekeeping Supervisor and Infection Control Officer.

### First Response Actions
Upon receiving a report of a rodent infestation, the following first response actions will be taken:
1. Seal food items to prevent contamination
2. Alert Infection Control to assess the risk of disease transmission
3. Deploy traps or bait to capture and eliminate the rodent
4. Schedule an urgent pest control treatment
5. Inspect food storage areas to prevent further contamination

### Escalation Chain
The escalation chain for rodent infestations is as follows:
1. Housekeeping Supervisor
2. Pest Control Vendor
3. Infection Control Officer
4. Hospital Administrator

### SLA Targets
The target response time for rodent infestations is 6 hours. The Pest Control team will aim to respond to all reports within this timeframe, with the goal of eliminating the infestation and preventing further contamination.

### Human Review Triggers
The following triggers will require human review and intervention:
* Reports of live rodents in patient wards or areas with high-risk patients (e.g. newborn or ICU areas)
* Reports of rodent droppings or gnaw marks in food storage or preparation areas
* Reports of chewed medical supplies or wires

### Audit Fields
The following fields will be audited to ensure compliance with this SOP:
* Date and time of report
* Location of infestation
* Type of rodent (if identified)
* Response time and actions taken
* Outcome of treatment and follow-up inspections
* Patient and staff feedback and concerns
